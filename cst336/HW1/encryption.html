What Is Encryption?

Encryption transforms readable data into an unreadable format known as ciphertext. Only those with the correct key can decrypt it. This process protects information in storage, during transmission, and even while being processed.

Types of Encryption

  • Symmetric Encryption: Uses one shared key for both encryption and decryption.
  • Asymmetric Encryption: Uses a public key to encrypt and a private key to decrypt.

Common Encryption Algorithms

  • AES: Advanced Encryption Standard used globally for data security.
  • RSA: A public-key system used for secure data exchange.
  • Twofish: Fast and flexible encryption for software and hardware use.

Importance of Encryption

Encryption protects privacy, prevents unauthorized access, and ensures safe online communication. It also helps organizations meet regulations such as HIPAA, PCI DSS, and GDPR.
