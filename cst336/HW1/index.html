What Is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting computers, networks, and data from unauthorized access or attack. It ensures the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information using a combination of technologies, policies, and best practices. In a world where most communication, banking, shopping, and healthcare are digital, cybersecurity has become an essential part of daily life.

Why Cybersecurity Matters

Cybersecurity impacts everyone—from individuals to large organizations. A single data breach can lead to identity theft, financial loss, or major service disruptions. Common cyber threats include malware, phishing attacks, and ransomware.

The goals of cybersecurity are often summarized in the CIA Triad:

  • Confidentiality: Ensuring only authorized users can access information.
  • Integrity: Keeping data accurate and unaltered by unauthorized parties.
  • Availability: Making sure information and systems are accessible when needed.

Common Cyber Threats

  • Viruses and Malware: Harmful software that can damage or steal data.
  • Encryption-Based Threats: Hackers may misuse encryption for ransomware or data theft.
  • Phishing: Deceptive messages that trick users into revealing personal information.

Each type of threat requires its own defense strategy, from antivirus protection to user awareness and strong data encryption.

Cybersecurity Best Practices

  • Keep software and operating systems up to date.
  • Use strong, unique passwords and enable multi-factor authentication (MFA).
  • Be cautious of suspicious emails, attachments, or links.
  • Back up your data regularly to a secure location.
  • Install and maintain antivirus and firewall software.
